NYU Go

Inspired by Amazon Go, NYU Go is an app designed to make easy and quick purchases with the click of a camera.

NYU Go initially requires you log in and once you have, it takes you direct access to submit an image or take one of a barcode. Once you have submitted an image, it reads the image and price and asks for you to confirm your purchase. When confirming, the user is taken to a page filled with his/her previous transactions.

Foodifai

Foodifai allows users to submit a photo either from their library or by taking a new photo. The photo is then analyzed and local restaurant recommendations are presented based on what is in the photo. The user can then request an Uber from within the app to get to where they want to go. Users never have to enter in a single line of text.

Cargo üöó February 2017

As part of an application proccess, I was given an exercise to "create a set of wireframes for the interface that makes selecting products and checking out fast, easy, and intuitive." The only information about the company available of the time was that the company's "user experience allowed passengers to choose products they want to purchase from their driver by using a mobile web app."

As a result, I modeled the application based on Tinder's swipe left, swipe right feature. To go from one panel to the next, the user can simply swipe right to procceed forward or swipe left to procceed back. I also made sure to stay consistent with the color scheme of the site to maintain company branding.
